# Engagement Risk Register — Lululemon Athletica (LULU) SEC 10-K Analysis

## Overview

This engagement risk register was developed through analysis of 
Lululemon Athletica's most recent SEC Form 10-K (2024 annual filing). 
The objective was to identify, classify, and assess ten distinct 
enterprise risks using established risk management frameworks, 
and to visualize the overall risk landscape using a risk matrix.

This exercise mirrors real-world second-line risk oversight activities 
in which risk analysts review organizational disclosures, regulatory 
filings, and operational documentation to identify control gaps, 
assess risk exposure, and support governance reporting.

---

## Methodology

1. Reviewed Lululemon's SEC Form 10-K (2024) as the primary source 
   document for risk identification
2. Identified ten material risks disclosed or implied within the filing
3. Classified each risk as **Pure** (insurable, downside only) or 
   **Speculative** (potential for gain or loss)
4. Assigned each risk to its primary **Risk Quadrant**: 
   Hazard, Operational, Financial, or Strategic
5. Scored each risk by **Likelihood** (Low / Medium / High) and 
   **Impact** (Low / Medium / High)
6. Constructed a **Risk Matrix** to visualize the overall risk landscape
7. Documented findings and supporting rationale for each risk assessment

## Key Risk Findings

### Data Security Breaches — High Likelihood / High Impact
Classified as a Pure Hazard risk. As a global retailer with extensive 
e-commerce operations, Lululemon maintains significant volumes of 
customer payment and personal data. A breach would trigger regulatory 
penalties under applicable data protection frameworks, damage brand 
trust, and result in substantial financial losses. This risk warrants 
priority mitigation and continuous monitoring.

### Supply Chain Disruptions — High Likelihood / High Impact
Classified as a Pure Operational risk. Lululemon's supply chain 
involves international manufacturing partners concentrated in specific 
geographic regions, creating exposure to geopolitical events, port 
disruptions, and logistics delays. Disruption would directly impact 
product availability and revenue generation.

### Regulatory Compliance — Medium Likelihood / High Impact
Classified as a Pure Operational risk. Lululemon operates across 
multiple jurisdictions with varying consumer protection, data privacy, 
and employment regulations. Non-compliance carries penalties, 
reputational damage, and operational disruption.

### Market Competition — High Likelihood / Medium Impact
Classified as a Speculative Strategic risk. Competitors including Nike, 
Gymshark, and Alo Yoga compete directly for Lululemon's core customer 
segment. While this risk creates potential for market share loss, it 
also incentivizes product innovation and brand differentiation.
